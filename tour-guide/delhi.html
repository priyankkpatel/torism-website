Historical Landmarks

  • Red Fort

    A magnificent fort built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648, the Red Fort is a stunning example of Mughal architecture.

  • Qutb Minar

    The tallest minaret in India, the Qutb Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a testament to the city's rich history.

  • Humayun's Tomb

    A beautiful tomb built by Haji Begum in 1565, Humayun's Tomb is a stunning example of Mughal architecture and a precursor to the Taj Mahal.

Cultural Attractions

  • Jama Masjid

    One of the largest mosques in India, the Jama Masjid is a stunning example of Mughal architecture and a popular cultural destination.

  • Sri Digambar Jain Lal Mandir

    A beautiful Jain temple that is home to a charity hospital for birds, the Sri Digambar Jain Lal Mandir is a unique cultural attraction.

  • Hauz Khas Village

    A vibrant cultural hub that is home to numerous art galleries, boutiques, and restaurants, Hauz Khas Village is a must-visit destination for culture enthusiasts.

Entertainment Options

  • Fun N Food Village

    A popular water park that offers a range of thrilling rides and attractions, Fun N Food Village is a great place to cool off during the summer months.

  • Delhi Art Gallery

    A popular art gallery that showcases a range of contemporary and modern art, the Delhi Art Gallery is a must-visit destination for art enthusiasts.
